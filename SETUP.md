# ApexFlight 3D - Advanced Realistic Flight Simulator

Welcome to **ApexFlight 3D**, an immersive, high-fidelity 3D airplane flight simulator built in Python. This simulator integrates rigid-body aeronautical physics, procedural 3D aircraft visual representations, interactive cockpit overlays, dynamic weather cycles, AI-controlled flight routing, and webcam-based gesture controls using OpenCV and MediaPipe.

---

## 1. Setup and Installation

### Prerequisites
- Python 3.8, 3.9, 3.10, or 3.11 (Python 3.10 is recommended)
- A working webcam for OpenCV gesture tracking (controls fall back automatically to keyboard if no camera is available)

### Installation Steps
1. Open a terminal (Command Prompt or PowerShell on Windows) in the workspace directory.
2. Install the required Python dependencies:
   ```bash
   pip install -r requirements.txt
   ```
3. Run the flight simulator:
   ```bash
   python flight_simulator/main.py
   ```

## 2. Flight Control Systems

ApexFlight 3D supports dual control methods: **OpenCV Gestures** and **Keyboard/Mouse**.

### A. OpenCV Webcam Gesture Controls (Immersive Pilot Mode)
When camera controls are enabled, the simulator spawns a telemetry feed window. The screen is split into left (throttle/flaps/gear) and right (yoke: pitch/roll) hemispheres relative to the pilot's physical hands:

| Control | Gesture (Detected by MediaPipe Hands) | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Roll (Bank)** | Tilt Right Hand left or right relative to center | Tilting hand horizontally rolls the plane. |
| **Pitch (Elevator)** | Raise or lower Right Hand relative to screen center | Raising hand pitches nose UP; lowering hand pitches nose DOWN. |
| **Yaw (Rudder)** | Tilt Right Hand fingers horizontally (left/right) | Yaw turns nose on ground during taxiing or trims tail in-flight. |
| **Throttle** | Left Hand vertical wrist position (slider) | Wrist at top of frame = 100% thrust. Bottom = 0% thrust. |
| **Flaps** | Left Hand finger count extended | 0-1 fingers = Flaps UP ($0^\circ$). 2-3 fingers = Flaps MID ($15^\circ$). 4-5 fingers = Flaps FULL ($30^\circ$). |
| **Landing Gear** | Squeeze Left Hand into fist / Open hand | Closed fist retracts landing gear. Open palm deploys gear. |
| **Brakes** | Hold Left Hand with only index finger extended (1 finger) | Engages landing brakes on ground. |
| **Cockpit Look** | Move head up/down or turn left/right (FaceMesh) | Turns pilot camera in cockpit, allowing looking out side windows. |

### B. Keyboard & Mouse Controls (Standard Flight Mode)
Keyboard controls are always active as a secondary input or fallback option:

- **Pitch (Elevator)**: `S` (Pitch Up / Pull Back), `W` (Pitch Down / Push Forward)
- **Roll (Ailerons)**: `A` (Roll Left), `D` (Roll Right)
- **Yaw (Rudder / Taxi Steering)**: `Q` (Yaw Left), `E` (Yaw Right)
- **Throttle**: `Left Shift` (Increase Power), `Left Control` (Decrease Power)
- **Flaps Cycle**: `F` (Cycles Flaps: Up -> Mid -> Full -> Up)
- **Landing Gear**: `G` (Toggles landing gear deployment)
- **Brakes**: `Space` (Hold to engage wheel brakes)
- **Cycle Cameras**: `V` (Cockpit view, Third-person chase, Wing view, Gear view, Free orbit)
- **Autopilot Master Modes**:
  - `X`: Toggle Altitude Hold (Cruise control)
  - `C`: Toggle Waypoint Path Navigation
  - `Z`: Engage Auto Takeoff Sequence
  - `L`: Engage ILS Auto Landing Sequence
- **Pause & Quit**: `Escape` (Exit flight, logs logbook, returns to hangar)

## 3. Aeronautical Physics Explanation

The flight dynamics engine (`aircraft_physics.py`) calculates forces acting on the aircraft center of gravity at 60 FPS:

### 1. Lift Force ($L$)
Lift is the upward force generated by air flowing over the wings:
$$L = \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 S C_L$$
- **$\rho$ (Air Density)**: Calculated using barometric formula $\rho = 1.225 \cdot e^{-h / 8500}$ (density decays exponentially as altitude $h$ increases, making flight harder in thin air).
- **$v$ (Airspeed)**: Velocity relative to the air mass (incorporates wind vector).
- **$S$ (Wing Area)**: Wing size of the selected aircraft.
- **$C_L$ (Lift Coefficient)**: Determined by the Angle of Attack (AoA) and flap settings:
  $$C_L = C_{L,\alpha} \cdot \text{AoA} + C_{L,\text{flaps}}$$

### 2. Aerodynamic Stall Mechanics
If the Angle of Attack (AoA) exceeds **15 degrees**, the airflow separates from the wing:
- The lift coefficient $C_L$ drops by **75%**.
- The drag coefficient $C_D$ spikes by **25%**.
- A nose-heavy pitching moment is applied, forcing the nose down (aerodynamic stall recovery design).
- Cockpit alarm sounds `STALL` warning, and wing buffering shakes the screen.

### 3. Drag Force ($D$)
Drag opposes forward motion and consists of parasite drag, induced drag, and configuration drag:
$$D = \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 S C_D$$
- **$C_D$ (Drag Coefficient)**:
  $$C_D = C_{D,0} + \frac{C_L^2}{\pi \cdot \text{AR} \cdot e} + C_{D,\text{gear}} + C_{D,\text{flaps}}$$
  - $C_{D,0}$: Base parasite drag.
  - $\frac{C_L^2}{\pi \cdot \text{AR} \cdot e}$: Induced drag (penalty for generating lift).
  - $C_{D,\text{gear}}$: Drag penalty when landing gear is deployed.
  - $C_{D,\text{flaps}}$: Drag penalty when flaps are extended.

### 4. Engine Spooling & Fuel weight
- Engines have realistic spool lag (first-order spooling inertia), meaning thrust does not immediately match throttle changes.
- Engine thrust degrades at high altitudes as air density decreases.
- Fuel is consumed based on throttle setting. As fuel decreases, the aircraft total mass decreases ($m = m_{\text{dry}} + m_{\text{fuel}}$), making the plane lighter, faster, and more responsive.

## 4. Open World Airport Systems

The simulator features two fully modeled airports:
1. **Metro International (KMSP)**:
   - Elevation: Sea level (0m).
   - Runway: 2500m concrete runway with white center markers.
   - Systems: Full visual approach lighting (ALS), runway edge lights (red/green ends, white borders), taxiways, terminal gate parking bays, and passenger boarding nodes.
2. **Mountain High Outpost (KASE)**:
   - Elevation: 300m above sea level, nestled in a valley.
   - Runway: 1200m short asphalt runway.
   - Approach: Challengingapproach path requiring sharp banking maneuver due to surrounding mountain peaks and severe wind shears.

## 5. Missions

Aviation missions are selectable from the main dashboard:
- **First Solo Takeoff**: Pilot the private jet. Takeoff, climb out to 2,000 feet, and maintain straight-and-level flight.
- **Storm ILS Landing**: Pilot the Cargo transporter. Start mid-air in zero visibility storm. Use the Instrument Landing System (ILS) localizer/glideslope HUD crosshairs to guide the heavy plane down onto runway 09R.
- **Engine Out Glide Challenge**: Pilot the Passenger jet. Start at 5,000 feet with 0 fuel (dual engine flameout). Manage airspeed, retract gear to reduce drag, glide back to Metro International, and perform a deadstick landing.
- **Fighter Interception**: Pilot the Fighter Jet. Test maximum G-forces, roll rates, and afterburner thrust in a high-speed interception training mission.
